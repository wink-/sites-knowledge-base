# Garden Inspiration — Rachel's Compact Grocery-Saving Garden

Source: https://youtu.be/3OFB441GWCg
Saved: April 2026

## What It Is

Rachel designed an 11x16 ft garden specifically to cut grocery costs. Compact, beginner-friendly, heavy on vertical growing with cattle panel trellises. Emphasis on high-value crops: eggs, berries, tomatoes, peppers, salad greens, herbs, squash.

## Key Design Elements

- **Chicken coop** (3x4 ft) in northwest corner, nesting boxes facing house for easy egg collection
- **Cattle panel arches** bent into trellises for vertical growing (tomatoes, beans, squash)
- **Compost bin** made from cattle panel, directly across from coop for easy cleaning
- **Concrete block raised beds** -- reconfigurable, holes useful for herbs and beneficial insect habitat
- **Dutch white clover** as living mulch (suppresses weeds, retains moisture, adds nitrogen)
- **Chicken run** enclosed with low fencing (36 sq ft for 4 chickens = 65-90 dozen eggs/year)

## Plant Layout

- Perennial bed: raspberries + strawberries (2 + ground cover)
- Arch trellis 1: 2 cherry tomatoes (Sun Gold, Super Sweet 100) + pole beans (Purple Podded)
- Arch trellis 2: Tromboncino squash (grows over coop, shades chickens, vine borer resistant)
- Grape vine on outside fence panel
- Hazelnuts espaliered on wire trellis (need 2 for pollination: Yamhill + Jefferson)
- 12 pepper plants (south side, full sun)
- 8 Swiss chard plants (north side, tolerates shade from hazelnuts)
- Salad greens under arches (spring: arugula/lettuce, fall: spinach/lettuce)
- Mediterranean herbs in warm concrete block holes (basil, rosemary, thyme)
- No-chicken alternative: blueberries in separate acidified bed (2 varieties)

## High-Value Crops Targeted

Eggs, berries, nuts, salad greens, peppers, grapes, tomatoes, green beans, summer squash, fresh herbs.

## What Rob Is Using From This

Rob's garden is modeled on this design:
- Raised beds already built and filled with compost
- Compost sourced from local municipal site (aged 1 year there + 1 year on site)
- Adding topsoil this growing season
- Winter garlic already planted
- Cattle panel experience (using for tree protection too)
- No chickens (yet?)

## Ideas for Rob's Garden

- **Add the cattle panel arches** for vertical growing -- already has cattle panels from tree fences
- **Dutch white clover between beds** -- low maintenance, fixes nitrogen
- **Cherry tomatoes + pole beans** -- high yield, easy, good for first real season
- **Skip the chickens for now**, focus on crops. Can always add later.
- **Tromboncino squash** -- vine borer resistant, huge producer, good for upstate NY
- **Herbs in the block holes** if using concrete block beds
- **Soil test first** -- compost + topsoil is great but pH matters for certain crops
